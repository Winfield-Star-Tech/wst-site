How to make money from the Corp


Dear Recruit,

How to make money from the corp

The whole idea behind Winfield Star-Tech is to create a corp which is mutually beneficial for its members, therefore the big question I am sure you are asking is how you can gain by being in the corp. At the moment this is by two main ways. By mining and by missioning.

MISSION RUNNING
Mission running fleets are often formed and allows players to take part in missions at higher levels than they currently have access to. I will personally arrange frequent level 2 and 3 mission fleets, from which all the rewards (including salvage) are split equally between the participants. When you join we will run a corp mission running op for you guys. This is a good chance to talk and get to know your corp mates. It is also a good introduction to mission running to get your feet wet. Usually other corp members are also happy to fleet up when running missions, so just ask in Corp chat.

MINING
Most of us use mining as our main source of income, as this provides a very good income and can be done semi afk. The Corporation is centered around this and the corp will buy any minerals you mine, the corp then uses these minerals to build ships and makes a profit from this. The corp then uses these profits to spend on facilities and projects etc. that benefit the members of the corp. There is usually someone 'boosting'. When someone is boosting for you it increases your mining capabilities greatly. We will also be running weekly wormhole ops; these give you access the rarer ores which are worth a lot of isk. We are also planning on running mining ops together. These include: A common ore op where we all mine together and split the income evenly. The common ore op will be good to get you a decent amount of isk and chat with your Corpmates. Weekly ops into a wormhole for rare ores to provide you with a large amount of isk and provide the corp with minerals. Finally we have the monthly ice mining op. We will run this once a month to provide our stations with fuel. Everyone in the corp participates and lasts over 3 days. You don't have to mine the whole time and with everyone taking part it goes by quickly. You do not get paid for this, but it helps the corp tremendously. Without it, the corp would have to buy and haul all its fuel, putting a large strain on the budget. Decreasing funding to planning events for you guys.

The first thing you will need to do is to mine ore. When you have mined the ore, you need to take it to Irgrus - Indigo-One. Here you can refine the ore into minerals but at the moment you probably won't have perfect refining skills, so instead you can create a contract to Taha Hakaari, setting the price etc. too free (more on how to do this below) and he will refine your ore into minerals with perfect refining skills and contract it back to you for free.

To create a contract to Taha, you need to click on 'E' on the upper left of your screen. Then select business-contracts. On the contracts screen select 'create contract'. On the next window ensure 'item exchange`, and `private` are selected. In the `Name` box put Taha Hakaari. Then click the `Next` button. on the next screen ensure the ore you want refined has a checkmark in the box next to it. Then select `next` again. On the next window ensure `I will pay` and `I will receive` are set to 0.0 and set the expiration to 2 weeks. Check all the details and select `Finish`. All refining will be done and sent back promptly.

When you have your minerals you can then sell them to the corp. This benefits both you and the corp as a whole and we encourage you to do so, as we need the minerals to keep our manufacturing running. The corp prices are good prices for the minerals to ensure you benefit by selling them to the corp and if done in Irgrus - Indigo-One it means you do not have to do lots of hauling runs to move your minerals to a market hub to sell them. To sell your minerals to the corp you will first need to look at the Corp Bulletin board, where you will see a list of `mineral blocks`. To do this go to the Corporation button on the left of your screen (the ringed planet with stars around it icon). On that select the `home` tab and then the `bulletins` tab. On there you will see the prices per unit the corp is currently buying minerals at and the list of `blocks`. The corp buys minerals from players in the form of those blocks for a set amount of ISK to ensure the corp has the correct ratios of minerals in stock for its manufacturing. The mineral prices are updated regularly.

Once you have the price set, you will need to create a contract in Irgrus - Indigo-One to the Corp. Again click on the 'E' in the upper-left part of the screen and go to Business then contracts. Ensure `Item Exchange` and 'My corporation` in the availability part. click 'next'. Again ensure all you want to sell has an 'mark' next to it. Click `Next` leave `I will pay` at 0.0 but then put the value you worked out your minerals cost in `I will receive`. Again set the `Expiration` to 2 weeks. check all the details and select `Finish`. Again contracts for Minerals will be checked and accepted promptly, if you have added up your minerals wrong it will be rejected. The mineral prices on the bulletin board do change, so check each time you set up a contract as the mineral values in Eve fluctuate.


I hope this information helps you to begin to make Isk whilst being in the corp. If anything is unclear, of course just ask in corp chat.

Ship Fits Part 2 (The Rules of Ship Fitting)



Dear Recruit,

1. Try not to mix your guns, go for guns of the same type and size and `group` them together they are much more effective this way. I see so many new players try to argue for a mix of gun types...trust me it is so much less effective; you will need to learn how to fly your ship to its best philosophy.

2. Use as many high slots as you can for weaponry, don't bother fitting a salvager as its more efficient to use a `salvage ship`.

3. Don't mix your tanks on your ships choose a type of tank, for those who don't know what Tanking means it's the primary way you `defend` your ship. Basically you either use Armor modules (Tend to be low slot modules) to bolster your ships armor OR you use Shield modules (Tend to be medium slot modules) to bolster your ships shields. Now each of Armor and Shield tanks can either be `Passive` (Means no modules need to be switched on to provide your defense) or `Active` (Means that a module has to be switched on which will actively repair the damage to your shields/armor). Example modules of Each type of tanking are as follows:

Passive Armor Tank: 100mm Reinforced Rolled Tungsten Plates I
Active Armor Tank: Small Armor Repairer I
Passive Shield Tank: Small Shield Extender I
Active Shield Tank: Small Shield Booster I

Again, please, please, please, remember never, never, never, mix your tanks. I have heard players say `well why don't I fit armor modules in my low slots and shield modules in my middle slots and get twice the amount of tanking? ` Well it isn't that simple as a properly tanked ship will use the `other` set of slots to bolster the effectiveness of its tanking modules as well as being used for resistance bonus modules and modules to add to your damage. And that's not even taking into consideration that you should fit rigs to your ship to bolster their tank. In a nutshell don't do it, I've often seen mixed tank fits posted when the ship has been lost and you will basically be a laughing stock.

4. Use Mission Specific hardeners and ammunition When you have the mission briefing window open try and see what type of enemy you are fighting. If you can see who the mission is against you can then look up the enemy using this link: http://www.ogrank.com/content/view/698/59 This will tell you what is the best type of damage to inflict on an enemy and what is best to tank. This can make a massive difference. You can then look at different ammunition types you can fit, look at the `show info` and its `attributes` and you will see what damage type the ammo does. Likewise, for the `tanking` modules I would advise you buy a resistance tanking module of each type for each resistance module you have fitted.

For example, if I had two shield resistance modules on a ship fit I would try to buy two of each of these: Limited 'Anointed' EM Ward Field Limited Explosive Deflection Field I Limited Kinetic Deflection Field I Limited Thermic Dissipation Field I Limited Adaptive Invulnerability Field I the last of these is a `universal` hardener that gives a lesser bonus to all the resistance types and can be very useful when an enemy can cause two damage types.

Armor resistance versions are: Prototype Energized EM Membrane I Prototype Energized Explosive Membrane I Prototype Energized Kinetic Membrane I Prototype Energized Thermic Membrane I and Prototype Energized Adaptive Nano Membrane I for the universal one. Armor tanking can also use these modules Prototype Armor EM Hardener I Prototype Armor Explosive Hardener I Prototype Armor Kinetic Hardener I Prototype Armor Thermic Hardener I (but they use up Capacitor unalike the other modules but give a greater bonus): (There is no `universal` one for these hardeners).

Please note there are different less and more expensive versions of all the above hardeners, they are just examples.

So if you see a fit which uses a hardener of some sort (and nearly all do) be prepared to `swap` different hardeners in and out to suit the needs of the mission you are on, again it's a brilliant habit to get into and is vital to survive higher level missions.

Ship Fits Part 3 (Meta Levels and Ships)


Dear Recruit,

Modules and Meta Levels

At first the wide range of modules that do the same thing, is at the very least bewildering. However, I will try to help you understand this and therefore be able to enhance your knowledge of the modules. The thing to consider id the `Meta Level` of the module. To see a Meta Level, go to `Show Info` and attributes. You can see it there. Now the simple general rule is the higher the Meta Level the better that module is at what it does, but in turn the more expensive it is. Now there is a useful function you can use to `compare` the abilities of a similar module. Go to `Show Info` on a module > variations and select the compare button. This will allow you to select different criteria (including metal levels) to compare modules of similar type. A guide to Metal Levels is as follows. Meta Levels 0 to 4 are considered T1 (Tech 1) modules, Meta Level 5 is called T2 (Tech 2) and Meta levels 6+ Are called Faction Modules. To begin with you will want to aim towards trying to fly mission running ships that are Meta 4 T1 fitted and then onto T2 fitted mission running ships. Eventually you will find yourself spending hundreds of millions of isk to fly a fully Faction Fitted ship.

Should I fit a Salvager?

Ok, many people will say (specially to begin with) `Why don't I just fit a Salvager or take and use Salvage drones so I can salvage my mission as I go? ` Well I would argue strongly against this. Salvagers take up a high slot these are the slots in which you mount your weapons. Why nerf your damage dealing capability to be able to salvage as you move through a mission? Even if you can't fit any more turrets or missile launchers in your high slots you can still add things like Energy Neutralizers which will help hinder the enemy. As for salvage drones, again, why not take Combat drones instead (useful hint always take 5 Light scout drones as no matter how big your ship you will find that these little beauties can be used against small fast ships like frigates that your bigger guns can't track). So what should you do to salvage? Well use a dedicated Salvage ship. Complete the objectives of your mission, then (before you tell the agent you have completed the mission) dock up, get into your salvage ship, tractor beam and salvage all the wrecks. It's a lot quicker than salvaging during a mission. Then when you are done, hand your mission in to your agent. Ultimately you will want to fly a Noctis to do this, with its bonus's to tractor beams and salvagers but to begin with until you get in one you can use a Destroyer hull very effectively to do this role. Trust me, in the long run it will save you a lot of time and effort (I've provided example Destroyer-Salvager fits for each of the races in my ship fits for you).

What ship for What Level of Mission?

There are a variety of different ship classes in Eve and with the ships and fits I have created for you as `starter fits` it is important to know for what level of mission the fits are aimed at. Essentially the rough guide is as below if you are soloing a mission with relatively low skills. The rough guide to the fits are as follows:

Frigates (Level 1 Security Missions)
Destroyers (Level 1 to 2 Security Missions)
Cruisers (Level 2 Security Missions)
Battlecruisers (Level 2 to 3 Security Missions)

Battleships can be used to solo level 3 missions and more experienced players can solo level 4's in them. I can but in all honesty due to the exponential risk in Level 4's I tend to only run them in fleets.
